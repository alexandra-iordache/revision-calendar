Why This Order? (ADHD Science)

This schedule is built on peer-reviewed research into how ADHD brains learn most effectively:
  • Interleaving, not blocking — Mixing subjects within each study day boosts long-term retention and maintains novelty, which ADHD brains need to stay engaged (Rohrer & Taylor, 2007).
  • Warm-up → Peak → Cool-down — Starting with the hardest topic causes avoidance paralysis. Instead, an accessible 25-min warm-up builds cognitive momentum, then the hard topic hits while you're "in the zone", and a satisfying review ends the day with a dopamine reward (Barkley, 2015).
  • Never the same module twice in a row — The primary (hardest) module changes every study day. This prevents the "boredom cliff" where ADHD focus collapses after sustained sameness (Zentall, 2005).
  • Formula ↔ Conceptual alternation — Days alternate between calculation-heavy (crypto, EVM) and conceptual (infra, security) hard tasks, varying the type of cognitive load.
  • Spaced repetition tags — Each new topic returns as a review 2, 5, and 7 days later. The spacing effect is even more critical for ADHD learners, who tend to lose retention faster without reinforcement.

Active Recall Techniques

Blurting

The Blurting Method

Read a topic for 10 min. Close everything. Write EVERYTHING you remember on a blank page. Open notes — use a different colour to fill gaps. The gaps are what to focus on next.

Flashcards

Active Recall Cards

Use Anki or physical cards. Front: question. Back: answer. Rate confidence 1-5. Wrong cards return sooner. For IY3660: every security definition + every block cipher step.

Teach It

Feynman Technique

Explain a concept out loud as if teaching a friend. If you stumble, you've found a gap. Essential for IY3612 — practise saying "brief, reasoned answers" aloud.

Past Papers

Exam Simulation

In Phases 2-3, do papers under timed conditions (2hrs). Mark yourself. This is the single best predictor of exam performance.

Your Ideal Study Day (ADHD-Optimised)

Each study day has 3 Pomodoro blocks (25+5 min each = 90 min total). The blocks follow the Warm-up → Peak Focus → Cool-down pattern. Each block uses a different module to maintain novelty and prevent the ADHD boredom cliff.
08:00

Morning Routine

Wake, shower, breakfast, coffee. No screens for 15 min — protects dopamine for study ahead.

09:00

BLOCK 1 — Warm-Up Module (25 min)

Start with something accessible and engaging — a lighter module, a review, or an interesting new topic. This builds cognitive momentum and overcomes ADHD start-up resistance. Never start with the hardest topic.

09:30

Short Break (5 min)

Quick stretch, water, look away from screen. Stay near your desk — you want to keep momentum.

09:35

BLOCK 2 — Peak Focus (25 min) — HARDEST topic

Now you're warmed up. Hit the hard topic: crypto formulas, graph theory, RSA proofs. Use Active Recall: close notes, write everything from memory, then check gaps. Your brain is primed from Block 1.

10:05

Big Break (20 min)

Walk, snack, stretch. Leave your desk completely. Movement resets ADHD focus for the final block.

10:25

BLOCK 3 — Cool-Down / Review (25 min)

End with a spaced repetition review or a lighter new topic from a third module. This gives your brain a dopamine reward (the satisfaction of recalling something) and makes you want to come back tomorrow.

11:00

Lunch + Life Admin (90 min)

Cook a proper meal. Do one chore: laundry, dishes, tidy. You live alone — staying on top of this prevents stress spirals.

12:30

OPTIONAL: Afternoon Session (repeat 3-block pattern)

If you're feeling good, do another 3-block cycle with different modules. Max 2 sessions per day (6 blocks total = 3 hrs focused study). If energy is low, skip this — consistency beats intensity.

14:00

STUDY DONE

Brain needs rest to consolidate. No guilt. You earned this.

14:00+

Free Time

Exercise, gaming, socialising, groceries, cooking, Netflix. One evening/week: batch-cook meals.

22:00

Wind Down (Optional)

10 min light flashcard skim. Reviewing before sleep boosts retention. But sleep > studying.
